How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
I interpret user needs by demanding hard data and strict acceptance criteria. Human communication is flawed and easy to misinterpret.
You cannot rely on vague verbal agreements. User stories help because they force requirements into a rigid format. 
They define exactly who needs the feature and why. Most importantly they require binary pass or fail metrics before any code is written.
This provides a hard paper trail of what "done" means and stops the team from building features based on bad assumptions.



How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach development iteratively to avoid the illusion of progress.
Building massive applications in a vacuum guarantees failure when requirements inevitably change.In my future work I will incorporate time-boxed sprints and strict definitions of ready and done.
I also prioritize daily scrums and early QA collaboration. Testing cannot be an afterthought at the end of the assembly line.I rely on hard velocity metrics rather than management pressure to plan capacity.



What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
A good team member operates with absolute transparency.You do not hide a lack of progress behind vague status updates.
You use information radiators to make your workflow visible and explicitly state what is blocking you.
It also means breaking down developer silos to take shared ownership of the product. Finally it means demanding written requirements to protect the team. 
If there is no documentation defining a finished feature the developers and testers inevitably take the blame when the client rejects the product.
